What is Waste?

Waste is anything we throw away that we don't need. But not all waste is the same! For effective management and a cleaner environment, it is crucial to sort our waste into three main categories. This simple step at home makes a huge difference for our communities and our planet.

Wet Waste

This includes all kitchen waste like leftover food, vegetable and fruit peels, tea leaves, and garden waste like leaves and flowers. It is biodegradable and can be turned into useful compost!

Dry Waste

This includes things like plastic bottles, paper, cardboard, glass, metal, and old clothes. These items can be recycled and reused, reducing the need to create new materials.

Hazardous Waste

This is waste that can be harmful to people and the environment. Examples include batteries, light bulbs, old medicines, and chemical cleaners. They need special care and disposal.
